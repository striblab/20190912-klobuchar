Measuring how Minnesota boosts Klobuchar's campaign

By Jeff Hargarten
Star Tribune September 12, 2019

As the crowded and competitive Democratic debate stage gradually winnows, Minnesota’s own Sen. Amy Klobuchar has soldiered on partially due to strong support from her home state.

The impact of Minnesota donors on Klobuchar’s campaign manifests in several ways, helping keep her in the presidential mix and setting her apart from her closest rivals months before either the crucial Iowa Caucus or the Super Tuesday voting deciding who gets the state’s delegates even begins.

Here’s some ways Minnesota has contributed to Klobuchar’s run so far:

Klobuchar leads in Minnesota small donors

Small donor data suggests some indecision among Minnesota Democrats as two dozen presidential candidates jockeys for position, yet Klobuchar maintains a healthy statewide lead in donations, cash and unique donors statewide, even over Sen. Bernie Sanders (D, VT) who overwhelmingly won the state caucus in 2016.

And while about a fifth of Klobuchar’s 20,000 unique donors also gave to other candidates, about 80 percent gave exclusively to her campaign.

Minnesota is Klobuchar's top donor source

About 79,000 unique donors have given small amounts to Sen. Amy Klobuchar's presidential campaign, with a quarter coming from Minnesotans, the most of any single state.

And while Klobuchar on average polls similarly to some candidates absent from Friday night’s debates, her donor count has kept her aloft so far, even as others have failed to qualify or dropped out entirely.

Klobuchar helps drive Minnesota enthusiasm

A combination of early Democratic enthusiasm, the raised profile of ActBlue as a donation tool and Klobuchar’s name in the ring are also likely factors driving Minnesota’s small donor engagement far past 2015’s pace.

Comparing ActBlue donations from Minnesotans between 2015 and 2019 shows an uptick in small donations to Democrats.
